1967 Russia Magical Realism

The Master and Margarita

by Mikhail Bulgakov

The Plot

Set in Soviet-era Moscow, a mysterious figure named Woland, who happens to be the Devil, arrives with his retinue, causing chaos and exposing the absurdities of the bureaucratic society. Meanwhile, we follow the love story between the Master, a besieged writer, and Margarita, who makes a pact with Woland to reunite with him. The narrative intertwines with a fantastical retelling of the trial of Jesus, blurring the lines between reality and fantasy as morality and desire collide.

The Buzz

This book has a bit of a cult following, hailed as a classic of Russian literature, although some readers find its shifts in narrative jarring. It’s a mix of absurdity and deep philosophical questions, with plenty of dark humor, making it one of those love-it-or-hate-it reads.

Why It Matters

Reading it now feels particularly relevant with its themes of repression, power dynamics, and the struggle for creative freedom. There’s a universality in the characters’ quests for love and meaning amidst chaos that resonates today.
